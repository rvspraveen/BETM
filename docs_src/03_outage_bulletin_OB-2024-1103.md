## FORCED OUTAGE: Fayette Power Project Unit 3 (FPP3) — 609 MW Coal

---

### 1. Event Summary

At **07:22 CPT on November 3, 2024**, Fayette Power Project Unit 3 (FPP3), a 609 MW coal-fired steam unit located near La Grange, Texas (ERCOT South Zone), experienced a **forced outage** due to a high-temperature trip on the high-pressure turbine stage. The unit was operating at 558 MW (91.6% output) at time of trip.

ERCOT Operations declared **Tight System Conditions** at 07:35 CPT and activated 1,200 MW of Non-Spinning Reserve Service (NSRS). Frequency reached a nadir of **59.94 Hz** at 07:25 CPT before recovering to normal operating range (59.97–60.03 Hz) by 07:31 CPT.

**Price Impact**

The RTM Settlement Point Price (HB_SOUTH) spiked to **$487/MWh** during the 07:15–07:30 interval before declining to $210/MWh as NSRS resources came online. HB_BUSAVG peaked at $312/MWh during the same interval.

---

### 4. ERCOT Operational Actions Taken

1. **07:23 CPT** — ERCOT Operations issued emergency AS deployment instruction (RRS/ECRS)
2. **07:28 CPT** — SCED re-dispatch committed 3 fast-start gas units: Barney Davis CT1 (150 MW), Lamar Power CT2 (185 MW), Victoria CT (90 MW)
3. **07:35 CPT** — Tight System Conditions declaration issued
4. **07:45 CPT** — Voltage support instruction issued for South Texas nuclear (STP Units 1&2) to increase reactive injection
5. **08:10 CPT** — NSRS stand-down partially; 600 MW released back to economic dispatch
6. **08:30 CPT** — Tight System Conditions lifted; ERCOT operating reserves exceeded 2,500 MW

---

### 5. Expected Return to Service

**Status as of 08:45 CPT, November 3, 2024:**

The QSE (Luminant Energy) has reported a **high-pressure turbine blade failure** requiring borescope inspection and potential blade replacement. Preliminary return date is **November 8, 2024**, subject to parts availability and borescope findings.

ERCOT will issue updated outage duration estimates as information becomes available. The unit will remain on forced outage status until the QSE submits a confirmed return-to-service notification via the ERCOT Outage Scheduler.

### 8. Corrective Action Requirements

Per ERCOT Protocols Section 6.5.5, the QSE (Luminant Energy QSE LLC) must submit:

1. **Preliminary Outage Report** within 24 hours of forced outage initiation (due: Nov 4, 07:22 CPT)
2. **Root Cause Analysis** within 30 days (due: Dec 3, 2024)
3. **Corrective Maintenance Plan** if root cause is attributable to deferred maintenance

ERCOT Market Monitoring will review the outage for compliance with forced outage reporting requirements.
