---

## SUBJECT: Expected High Congestion on Panhandle-to-North 345kV Interface — Operating Days October 15–19, 2024

---

### 1. Summary

ERCOT operations staff anticipates **sustained binding congestion** on the Panhandle-to-North 345kV transmission interface (hereinafter "PH-N Interface") during peak wind generation hours on October 15–19, 2024, due to the coincidence of:

1. High West Texas wind output (forecast 12,000–15,500 MW, representing ~85–95% of installed Panhandle capacity);
2. The ongoing outage of the **Tule Canyon 345/138kV autotransformer #2** (Outage ID: OUT-2024-7731, expected return: October 20, 2024);
3. Above-normal North Zone load following the early-season cold front forecast to arrive October 15.

Participants should expect **significant LMP divergence** between West Texas nodes (HB_WEST, HB_PAN, LZ_WEST) and North Zone nodes (HB_NORTH, LZ_NORTH), with preliminary modeling suggesting spreads of **$45–$120/MWh** during hours 01:00–08:00 and **$0–$25/MWh** during on-peak hours when system load absorbs excess generation.

---

### 4. CRR Holder Advisory

CRR holders with **West-to-North** positions (source: HB_WEST or HB_PAN; sink: HB_NORTH or LZ_NORTH) may receive **significant positive congestion component payments** during the off-peak window.

CRR holders with **North-to-West** positions should be aware of potential obligations during the affected period.

**CRR Account Holders are reminded** that congestion component payments in the Day-Ahead Market are settled against DAM SPPs, not Real-Time SPPs. Basis risk between DAM and RTM congestion remains.

---

### 5. Wind Resource Curtailment Protocols

West Texas wind QSEs should be prepared for potential **Dispatch Instructions (DIs)** reducing output below HASL during congested hours. Resources will be dispatched economically per SCED; however, if SCED re-dispatch alone cannot resolve the constraint, ERCOT may issue manual curtailments.

Wind resources receiving curtailment DIs are reminded:
- Non-compliance with DIs may result in an Uninstructed Deviation charge
- Resources curtailed for reliability (RUC commitment conflicts) will receive RUC Guarantee protection per Section 4.6 of the ERCOT Protocols

---

### 6. Load Forecast Context

The early-season cold front arriving October 15 is expected to drive North Zone load to **42,500–44,200 MW** during HE 08–10, compared to the 5-year October average of 38,900 MW. ERCOT's Load Forecast team is monitoring closely and will issue an updated load forecast by October 14, 20:00 CPT.

### 7. Recommended Actions for QSEs

1. **Review DAM offers** for West Texas resources during HE 01–08. Negative LMPs are possible; ensure offer floors are appropriate.
2. **Update CRR positions** if your hedge does not match expected physical flows.
3. **Verify COAS for North Zone resources**: Reliability re-dispatch may create intra-day commitment opportunities.
4. **Monitor ERCOT Nodal messaging system** for real-time constraint updates and supplemental DIs.
5. **Prepare load resource bids** in LZ_NORTH if you have curtailable load — emergency AS deployment is possible if load peaks above forecast.
